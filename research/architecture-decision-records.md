# architecture decision records

> Generated by /ae-research → research on 2026-06-25

## Summary
An Architecture Decision Record (ADR) is a short document that captures a single architecturally significant decision along with its context, rationale, trade-offs, and consequences, so future developers understand the "why" behind a design choice. The concept originates from Michael Nygard's 2011 essay, whose five-section template (Title, Status, Context, Decision, Consequences) remains the foundational format; MADR (Markdown ADR) is the leading modern alternative, adding explicit considered-options and pros/cons analysis. Multiple primary authorities — adr.github.io, the canonical GitHub repos (joelparkerhenderson, architecture-decision-record), Google Cloud, and Nygard's original blog — converge on this definition with no meaningful dispute. A collection of all ADRs for a project forms an Architecture Decision Log (ADL), and a mature tooling ecosystem exists, led by npryce/adr-tools (bash, Nygard format, Markdown in doc/adr) with ports in at least eight languages and multi-format generators. A 2026 empirical study (arXiv:2604.27333) comparing five templates found MADR scored highest in expert analysis (0.900) but Nygard significantly outperformed MADR in a controlled student experiment, concluding template choice should be context-dependent rather than one-size-fits-all.

  - Evidence: Four independent primary sources converge near-verbatim. adr.github.io: 'An ADR captures a single AD and its rationale.' The GitHub canonical repos: 'a document that captures an important architecture decision made along with its context and consequences,' with 'Each ADR should be about one AD, not multiple ADs.' Nygard's originating 2011 essay defines it as 'a short text file' for one significant decision. All four claims (0, 3, 10, 18) state the same definitional fact and unanimously passed verification; merged here with combined primary sourcing.
  - Sources: https://adr.github.io/, https://github.com/architecture-decision-record/architecture-decision-record, https://github.com/joelparkerhenderson/architecture-decision-record, https://cognitect.com/blog/2011/11/15/documenting-architecture-decisions
- **An Architectural Decision (AD) is a justified design choice addressing an architecturally significant requirement — one affecting structure, non-functional characteristics, dependencies, interfaces, or construction techniques.** — confidence: high
  - Evidence: adr.github.io defines an AD verbatim as 'a justified design choice that addresses a functional or non-functional requirement that is architecturally significant.' The architecture-decision-record glossary: AD is 'a software design choice that addresses a significant requirement.' Nygard's essay enumerates architecturally significant decisions as 'those that affect the structure, non-functional characteristics, dependencies, interfaces, or construction techniques.' Definitional, stable terminology (Kruchten/Zimmermann/Nygard lineage), corroborated by Wikipedia and MADR docs.
  - Sources: https://adr.github.io/, https://github.com/architecture-decision-record/architecture-decision-record, https://cognitect.com/blog/2011/11/15/documenting-architecture-decisions
- **The purpose of ADRs is to convey the reasons, trade-offs, and consequences behind a chosen decision, establishing shared team understanding and helping future developers understand the rationale.** — confidence: high
  - Evidence: adr.github.io: ADRs 'help you understand the reasons for a chosen architectural decision, along with its trade-offs and consequences.' joelparkerhenderson repo: 'decision records are a way for teams to think smarter and communicate better' and are created 'when we want future developers to understand the why of what we're doing.' Corroborated by Nygard, Fowler, and AWS Prescriptive Guidance. Note: literature also flags that ADRs can drift or become after-the-fact paperwork in practice, but this concerns adherence, not the stated purpose.
  - Sources: https://adr.github.io/, https://github.com/joelparkerhenderson/architecture-decision-record
- **A collection of all ADRs for a project forms an Architecture Decision Log (ADL).** — confidence: high
  - Evidence: Primary source defines ADL verbatim as 'the collection of all ADRs created and maintained for a particular project (or organization).' Single primary source but unanimous vote and corroborated by AWS Prescriptive Guidance summaries; low-stakes definitional claim.
  - Sources: https://github.com/architecture-decision-record/architecture-decision-record
- **The original Nygard ADR template (Michael Nygard, 2011) structures a decision with five sections: Title, Status, Context, Decision, and Consequences.** — confidence: high
  - Evidence: Nygard's 2011 Cognitect essay enumerates exactly these five sections (Context = 'forces at play, including technological, political, social, and project local'; Status = proposed/accepted/deprecated/superseded; Consequences = 'the resulting context'). adr.github.io/adr-templates: 'Based on Michael Nygard's 2011 work, this format structures decisions with: title, status, context, decision, and consequences.' Corroborated by joelparkerhenderson repo, Fowler's bliki, arc42. Minor nit: the original template orders Status before Decision, but the claims do not assert ordering.
  - Sources: https://cognitect.com/blog/2011/11/15/documenting-architecture-decisions, https://adr.github.io/adr-templates/
- **MADR (Markdown Architectural Decision Records) is a streamlined template for recording architecturally significant decisions, distinguished by treating considered options with their pros and cons as essential.** — confidence: high

Corroborated by the github.com/adr/madr repo, ozimmer.ch primer, and a peer-reviewed CEUR-WS paper. MADR is actively maintained (4.x).
  - Sources: https://adr.github.io/madr/, https://adr.github.io/adr-templates/

  - Evidence: Google Cloud primary doc verbatim: 'An ADR captures the key options available, the main requirements that drive a decision, and the design decisions themselves.'   - Sources: https://cloud.google.com/architecture/architecture-decision-records
- **ADRs should be created when there is a technical challenge with no existing basis for a decision, an undocumented solution, or two-or-more competing engineering options to document.** — confidence: high
  - Evidence: Google Cloud doc lists all three criteria nearly verbatim. Compatible with AWS Prescriptive Guidance, Microsoft Azure Well-Architected, and Fowler (architecturally significant, hard-to-reverse decisions). Single vendor source for the specific phrasing but corroborated in spirit and unanimously verified.
  - Sources: https://cloud.google.com/architecture/architecture-decision-records

  - Sources: https://arxiv.org/html/2604.27333v1

  - Evidence: Paper states template selection 'should not follow a one-size-fits-all approach' but should depend on 'specific architectural context, time constraints, and documentation goals.' Accurate characterization; 'high-stakes' is a mild interpretive gloss for structural/rationale richness. Medium confidence due to reliance on the single preprint.

## Caveats
The definitional and tooling findings rest on strong, mutually corroborating primary sources (adr.github.io, the canonical GitHub repos, Nygard's 2011 essay, vendor docs) and are high-confidence and stable since 2011. The empirical comparison findings (claims 14-17) all derive from a SINGLE unpublished arXiv preprint (arXiv:2604.27333, submitted April 2026): the 'expert' evaluators were the paper's own two authors, the controlled experiment used a small sample of 33 undergraduate students, and the Nygard-over-MADR result is specific to the 'Overall Score' dimension — so these results should be treated as preliminary, not settled findings. The arXiv ID's high sequence number initially looked malformed but was confirmed real via multiple fetches. The multi-format tooling claim (23) passed only 2-1 and conflates capabilities across several distinct tools rather than one tool. Vendor-template specifics (Google Cloud, claims 5-7) are accurate to that source but are broader than the classic Nygard/MADR formats and should not be generalized as the universal ADR structure. Some web sources surfaced practitioner criticism that ADRs drift from purpose or become after-the-fact paperwork — a real-world adherence concern not reflected in the definitional claims.

## Open questions
- Will the arXiv:2604.27333 findings (Nygard outperforming MADR in practice) hold up under peer review and replication with practitioner samples rather than 33 undergraduates?
- What is the documented best practice for handling ADR lifecycle states (proposed, accepted, deprecated, superseded) and linking superseding decisions over time, beyond the bare status field?
- How widely are ADRs actually adopted and maintained in industry, and what failure modes (drift, abandonment, after-the-fact rationalization) most commonly undermine them?
- How do ADRs integrate with formal architecture standards such as ISO/IEC/IEEE 42010, and what does the '42010-inspired' template format concretely add over Nygard/MADR?
